About me

Designing data-driven resilience for infrastructure systems

My research blends physics-based simulation, machine learning, and collaborative sensing to detect emerging risks for structural and transportation assets. I work with open data practices to translate lab-scale insights into real-world priorities for communities and practitioners.

Alongside research, I lead collaborative sensing platforms and open-source tools that let engineers and citizens co-create resilient systems.

Research Focus

  • Climate-adaptive digital twins that steer resilient decisions for transportation and energy corridors.
  • Physics-aware machine learning models that retain interpretability under sparse instrumentation.
  • Community-centered sensing platforms and open-source tools that invite collaboration across engineering teams.
