Research Interests

My research explores conservation genetics of marine life with an applied focus on aquaculture and sustainable fishing in the U.S. This work blends population genomics, digital twins, and machine learning to detect emerging threats and translate lab-scale insights to real-world assets.

I aim to build interpretable, physics-aware models that remain robust under sparse data while empowering practitioners and communities through open-source sensing platforms and transparent dashboards.

Ongoing Research

Selected projects shepherding experiments into resilient practice.

1. Population structure of the eastern oyster

The eastern oyster is a linchpin of resilient estuarine ecosystems. My work merges genomics, habitat surveys, and collaborative sensing to reveal how breeding programs and restoration efforts shape connectivity across critical habitats.

2. Quantifying oyster shell bioerosion with x-ray techniques

Partnering with the Coastal Disease Ecology Lab to map microbial and physical weathering of shells. High-resolution x-ray imaging feeds physics-aware machine learning models that anticipate vulnerability and inform adaptive aquaculture strategies.
